The role-playing video game genre began in the mid-1970s inspired by a pen-and-paper role-playing games that was Dungeons & Dragons.

;

Several other sources of inspiration for early role-playing video games also included fantasy writings by authors such as J. R. R. Tolkien, chess and literature of the Epic of Gilgamesh which inspired the famous 'hero's journey'.

>

After the success of role-playing video games such as Ultima and Wizardry, which in turn served as the blueprint for Dragon Quest and Final Fantasy.

>

the role-playing genre eventually diverged into two styles, Eastern role-playing games and Western role-playing games, due to cultural differences

>

Broadly speaking, JRPGs have become known more for having pre-defined characters with set traits and personalities, whereas Western RPGs tend toward customizable characters.
